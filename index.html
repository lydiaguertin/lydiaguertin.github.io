A pulsar is a highly magnetised rotating neutron star that emits beams of electromagnetic radiation from its magnetic poles.

Discovery

The first pulsar was detected in 1967 by Jocelyn Bell Burnell. Its regular pulses were so precise that astronomers briefly considered it might be an extraterrestrial signal — they called it LGM-1.
